## Why teams look for a Gmelius alternative for mail merge

Gmelius is positioned around shared inboxes, email collaboration, and workflow automation for teams. Mail merge is part of the picture, but it is one capability among many. Common reasons people look for an alternative focused on merging:

- they want personalized mass email as the primary job, not a side feature;
- they already keep recipients in Google Sheets and send from Gmail;
- they don't need shared-inbox or kanban-style collaboration tooling;
- they want a simpler workflow and more predictable pricing for a small team;
- they prefer a tool built specifically around Sheets-to-Gmail personalization.

The question is not "which suite does the most?" It is: **which tool makes a clean, personalized Sheets-to-Gmail campaign the thing it does best?**

## Personalizing from Google Sheets columns (workflow)

1. **Set up your recipient sheet.** Columns for email, first name, company, and any custom fields you want to merge.
2. **Clean the list.** Remove duplicates and invalid addresses; keep only permissioned, relevant contacts.
3. **Write a concise template.** Insert merge fields where they add real context.
4. **Preview multiple rows.** Watch for empty fields so nothing reads as generic.
5. **Send a test, then your segment.** Confirm rendering before the full send.
6. **Track and follow up.** Use engagement to plan a timely next touch from the same Sheet.

## Attachments, open/click tracking & automated follow-ups

When your message needs a file — a brochure, contract, or one-pager — attachment sends keep everything together. Open and click tracking show who engaged so follow-ups go to the right people. And because the Sheet is your source of truth, planning the next email — to non-openers or repliers — stays contextual and simple.

## Sending limits & deliverability best practices

Gmail and Google Workspace enforce daily sending limits that no add-on removes. To protect deliverability:

- send to permissioned, relevant contacts;
- warm up volume gradually instead of blasting a cold list;
- keep copy human and specific; avoid spammy phrasing;
- include opt-out language where appropriate and honor it;
- test before sending and watch bounces and replies.
