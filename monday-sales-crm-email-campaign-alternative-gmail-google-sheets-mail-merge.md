## When a Sheet-to-Gmail campaign is simpler than a CRM campaign setup

Use a Gmail + Sheets mail merge when:

- the audience is a focused segment, not a long-running marketing list;
- a salesperson, founder, recruiter, or account manager wants to review every row;
- your personalization context is easiest to edit in Google Sheets;
- replies should land in Gmail and be handled manually;
- you want a lightweight campaign without rebuilding CRM automation for one send.

If your team already manages email governance, subscription status, dashboards, and team ownership inside a CRM, keep that system as the source of truth. Mail Merge is best for the campaign slice that benefits from a spreadsheet review layer.

## When monday sales CRM or a CRM email tool is still the better fit

Choose a CRM-native workflow when you need pipeline records, board automation, multi-user permissions, full CRM reporting, campaign lifecycle management, or a system of record for every account interaction. A spreadsheet mail merge should not be used to bypass required CRM governance, subscription controls, or account-history rules.

## Export or prepare your contact segment in Google Sheets

Start with the smallest useful segment. Keep the CRM export or working Sheet reversible: preserve IDs, owner fields, and stage/status columns so you can update the CRM later through your normal process.

Suggested columns:

| Column | Example | Why it helps |
|---|---|---|
| `email` | alex@example.com | Required recipient field |
| `first_name` | Alex | Greeting personalization |
| `company` | Northstar Co | Account context |
| `owner` | Priya | Internal ownership |
| `deal_stage` | Demo completed | Relevant context |
| `reason_to_write` | asked about rollout timing | Human-reviewed personalization |
| `cta` | book a 15-minute review | Clear next step |
| `opt_out_or_dnc` | false | Suppression before sending |
| `send_status` | ready | Batch control |
| `crm_update_needed` | yes | Follow-up after replies |

Remove unsubscribed, bounced, duplicate, do-not-contact, or unclear records before you load the campaign.

## Write a Gmail draft with safe merge fields and send a test

Keep the message specific but not creepy. Merge only fields you trust.

Subject: `Quick follow-up for {{company}}`

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> I saw {{company}} is currently at {{deal_stage}} and wanted to follow up because {{reason_to_write}}.
>
> Would {{cta}} be useful this week?
>
> Best,
> {{owner}}

Before sending, test multiple rows: a perfect row, a row with a missing optional field, and a row from a different segment. Check links, attachments, sender identity, subject line, and any opt-out language.

## Launch in reviewed batches and update CRM status after replies

A safe campaign process is operational, not just technical:

1. Filter to rows marked `ready`.
2. Send a test to yourself and a teammate.
3. Start with a small batch.
4. Monitor bounces, replies, complaints, and out-of-office responses.
5. Mark `sent_at`, `replied`, `bounced`, `opted_out`, and `follow_up_needed` in the Sheet.
6. Update important outcomes back in monday sales CRM or your CRM through the process your team already trusts.

Respect Gmail and Google Workspace sending limits. Use consented or clearly relevant contacts, avoid purchased/spammy lists, include opt-out language where appropriate, and never send rows that are marked unsubscribed or do-not-contact.

## monday sales CRM email campaign alternative FAQ

### Is Mail Merge for Gmail and Sheets a full monday sales CRM replacement?

No. Mail Merge is a lightweight Gmail + Google Sheets campaign workflow. It does not replace a CRM system of record, pipeline management, team permissions, reporting, integrations, or automation.

### Can I use a monday sales CRM export with Gmail mail merge?

Yes, if your team is allowed to export or prepare the segment. Preserve IDs and owner/status columns, remove suppressed contacts, clean the Sheet, map safe fields into a Gmail draft, and update CRM records after replies.

### When is a CRM-native email campaign better?

Use the CRM-native route when you need governed subscriber status, CRM dashboards, shared team workflows, automations, lifecycle reporting, or strict account-history controls.

### What columns should I add before sending?

Start with email, first name, company, owner, stage, reason to write, CTA, opt-out/do-not-contact status, send status, reply status, and CRM update needed.

### How do I keep a Gmail campaign deliverable?

Send only to consented or clearly relevant contacts, avoid purchased lists, test before launch, keep copy honest, respect Gmail/Workspace limits, monitor bounces and replies, and honor opt-outs promptly.

## Start from the segment you already trust

If the audience is already filtered and ready for human review, Google Sheets can be the campaign control panel and Gmail can be the sender. Keep the CRM for source records; use Mail Merge for the focused send.
