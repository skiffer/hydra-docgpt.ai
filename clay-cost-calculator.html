Why Clay gets expensive — and how Sheets is cheaper

Clay's monthly fee is only the entry ticket. Real enrichment spends credits per action — find email, verify it, find phone, pull firmographics — and a "waterfall" can hit several paid providers per contact. That's why a 500-contact full-enrichment run can cost hundreds in credits on top of a $495/mo plan.

GPT for Sheets is a lightweight Clay alternative inside Google Sheets: you connect the same data providers (Hunter, Prospeo, LeadMagic, Apollo) with your own API keys and call them with formulas like =HUNTER_DOMAIN_SEARCH() or =LEADMAGIC_FIND_EMAIL(). You pay each provider directly at their rate — no platform markup — on a flat add-on subscription.

FAQ

How much does Clay really cost per contact?

Beyond the subscription, basic enrichment is ~14 credits (about $0.22–$1.05 depending on plan) and full enrichment ~34 credits (about $0.54–$2.55). Multi-provider waterfalls increase this further — which is why the per-contact cost dominates the bill at scale.

Is GPT for Sheets a Clay alternative?

Yes — it runs lead enrichment, company research, and email finding/verification directly in Google Sheets using the same providers, at a flat subscription plus your own (cheaper) provider keys.

Do I need my own API keys?

For enrichment functions (Business plan), yes — you add each provider's key, which is what keeps the per-contact cost low. AI formulas (=GPT, =CLAUDE…) include credits and don't require a key.
