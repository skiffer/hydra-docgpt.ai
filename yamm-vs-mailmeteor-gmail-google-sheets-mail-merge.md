## YAMM vs Mailmeteor at a glance

Both YAMM and Mailmeteor are mature Google Workspace add-ons that run mail merges from a Google Sheet and send through Gmail. They overlap heavily; the differences are mostly in interface, tracking presentation, plan structure, and the surrounding details.

| Personalization | Merge fields from Sheet columns | Merge fields from Sheet columns |
| Tracking | Opens/clicks/replies reported back to the Sheet | Open/click tracking and reporting |
| Follow-ups | Supported within the workflow | Supported within the workflow |
| Sending | Through your Gmail/Workspace account | Through your Gmail/Workspace account |

## How each personalizes from Google Sheets

The mental model is the same across both: your spreadsheet is the source of truth, each row is a recipient, and column headers become merge fields you drop into the email body and subject. The practical differences come down to how previews are presented, how easily you can spot empty fields, and how the add-on reports results back into the Sheet. If you already think in terms of "one row per recipient, one column per field," either tool — or a simpler Sheets-native add-on — will feel familiar.

## Attachments, tracking & follow-ups compared

Both YAMM and Mailmeteor offer open and click tracking and support follow-up sends; exact behavior (for example, how attachments or per-recipient files are handled, and how non-openers are segmented) varies by plan and changes over time. The decision usually hinges on which reporting view you find clearest and whether the follow-up flow matches how you actually work, rather than on a single missing feature.

## Sending limits & deliverability

Neither add-on removes Gmail or Google Workspace daily sending limits — those are set by Google, not the tool. Whichever you pick, the deliverability fundamentals are identical: send to permissioned, relevant contacts; warm up volume gradually; keep copy specific and human; include opt-out where appropriate; and test before each send. A tool can make sending easier, but responsible list and copy habits are what protect your inbox placement.

## Pricing models compared (factual, dated)

As of June 2026, both YAMM and Mailmeteor use tiered pricing with free entry tiers and paid plans that raise daily recipient limits and unlock features; the exact numbers, quotas, and tier names change, so verify current pricing on yet-another-mail-merge.com and mailmeteor.com. Rather than chasing the lowest sticker price, compare the daily volume you actually need and the features you will genuinely use.
