Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a Google Workspace account to use Slides AI by DocGPT.AI?

Yes, you need a Google account to use Slides AI by DocGPT.AI as it integrates directly with Google Slides. However, a free Gmail account is sufficient - you don't need a paid Google Workspace subscription.

How do I launch Slides AI by DocGPT.AI from Google Slides?

After installation, open any Google Slides presentation, click on "Extensions" in the menu bar, select "Create Presentation with AI ChatGPT | Slides™" and click "Launch". The sidebar will open where you can start creating presentations.

Can I cancel my subscription at any time?

Yes, you can cancel your subscription at any time. Your access will continue until the end of your current billing period, and you won't be charged again.

Do I need to create a separate account?

No separate account is needed. The add-on uses your existing Google account for authentication and access.

What's the difference between Slides AI by DocGPT.AI and SlidesAI?

The main differences are: DocGPT.AI offers unlimited presentations on the Pro plan while SlidesAI limits you to 120/year on their Pro plan. DocGPT.AI also offers a lifetime plan for $249, while SlidesAI doesn't have this option. However, SlidesAI supports both Google Slides and PowerPoint, while DocGPT.AI is Google Slides only.

How can I contact customer support?

We offer online support through our help center and email support. Professional and higher plans also receive priority support with faster response times.

Is the lifetime plan really unlimited?

Yes! The $249 lifetime plan gives you unlimited presentations for life with all features included. It's a one-time payment with no recurring charges, making it the best value for long-term users.

Which tool is better for me?

Choose Slides AI by DocGPT.AI if you need unlimited presentations, want a lifetime plan option, or prefer better value for money. Choose SlidesAI if you need PowerPoint support, want to try a free plan first, or only create presentations occasionally (less than 10/month).
