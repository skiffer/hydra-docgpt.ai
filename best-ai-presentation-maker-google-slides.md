## Short answer {#short-answer}

The "best AI presentation maker for Google Slides" depends on one question first: **do you want the AI inside Google Slides, or are you willing to build decks on a separate platform and export?**

If you live in Google Slides — your team collaborates there, your decks belong in Drive, your sharing and version history matter — an **add-on** is the right shape of tool: **Slides by DocGPT.ai, Plus AI, MagicSlides, and SlidesAI** all generate native, editable Slides decks. Of those, [Slides by DocGPT.ai](/google-slide-ai/) pairs the most generous stated free tier (10 presentations/month) with a straightforward topic-to-deck workflow, 40+ languages, and paid plans from $14.99/mo.

If you're open to a standalone platform, **Gamma** makes striking card-style web decks and **Beautiful.ai** automates design polish — both export to PPTX that you can then import into Google Slides, but the editing happens on their platform, not in Slides.

## Tool-by-tool verdicts {#verdicts}

### Slides by DocGPT.ai — best if you live in Google Slides

Slides by DocGPT.ai generates a complete deck from a topic or your own pasted text, with slide count, writing tone, and 40+ output languages under your control. Because it's a Workspace add-on, the result is a native, editable Google Slides file in your Drive from the first second — collaboration, comments, sharing, versioning, and PPTX/PDF export all work exactly as Slides always has. The free tier covers 10 presentations a month and paid plans start at $14.99/mo ([pricing](/google-slide-ai/#pricing)). It's part of the DocGPT.ai suite used by 500,000+ users. The honest limitation: it's Google Slides-only — if your organization presents from PowerPoint, look at Plus AI or SlidesAI. [Install it here](https://workspace.google.com/marketplace/app/create_presentation_with_ai_chatgpt_slid/517982554715).

### Plus AI — best template polish among the add-ons

Plus AI runs as an extension in both Google Slides and PowerPoint and has earned a reputation for professional, consistent templates. Its Pro plan ($20/mo billed annually) adds document uploads, prompts beyond 100K characters, and AI image generation; the Team plan adds brand controls and shared libraries. The trade-off is entry cost: there's no permanent free plan listed — just a 7-day trial — with Basic at $10/mo billed annually or $15 month-to-month. Full breakdown: [Plus AI vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/plus-ai-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### MagicSlides — best for converting existing content into slides

MagicSlides' signature is input breadth: its site advertises converting text, topics, YouTube videos, PDFs, URLs, images, and audio into Google Slides decks, and claims 2.3M+ users. Premium is $29/mo (50 presentations/month, up to 50 slides each); Premium Plus is $34/mo for unlimited presentations. It's free to start without a card, though the pricing page doesn't state current free limits. If your decks usually begin from a YouTube video or a PDF, it's worth a trial. Full breakdown: [MagicSlides vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/magicslides-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### SlidesAI — cheapest paid add-on entry point

SlidesAI works with both Google Slides and PowerPoint and has the lowest published paid price of the add-ons here: Pro at $10/mo (120 presentations/year, 6,000-character input) and Premium at $20.83/mo billed annually for unlimited presentations. Its free plan is real but thin — 12 presentations per year with a 2,500-character input cap — which suits genuinely occasional users. Full breakdown: [SlidesAI vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/slidesai-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### Gamma — best for card-style web decks, not a Slides tool

Gamma is a standalone platform, and a genuinely good one: its card-based format produces modern, scroll-friendly decks and websites, and it exports to PPTX, PDF, and PNG. But it does not work inside Google Slides — you create and edit on gamma.app, and free-plan AI credits are one-time (they don't refresh; free exports carry a "Made with Gamma" badge, removed on the Plus tier). Its Plus and Pro tiers add monthly credits and API access — see gamma.app for current prices. Choose Gamma when the web-native deck format is the point; choose an add-on when Slides is. Full breakdown: [Gamma vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/gamma-app-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### Tome — discontinued

Tome shut down its AI presentation product on April 30, 2025, after announcing a pivot away from presentations; the team moved on to a sales-intelligence product. It's listed here because it still appears in older "best AI presentation tool" roundups — if you're migrating away from it, any of the add-ons above will keep your decks in a format you own. Historical comparison: [Tome vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/tome-app-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### Beautiful.ai — best design automation on its own platform

Beautiful.ai's smart templates reflow layouts as you add content, which keeps non-designers within professional guardrails better than almost anything else. It's a standalone platform (with PowerPoint import/export and PDF export), not a Slides add-on, and there's no free plan — a 14-day trial that requires a credit card, then Pro at $12/mo billed annually or a $45 single-presentation option. Choose it for design automation; skip it if you need to stay inside Google Slides. Full breakdown: [Beautiful.ai vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/beautiful-ai-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

### Presentations.ai — standalone with a usable free starter

Presentations.ai is a standalone web platform with iOS and Android apps, exporting to PowerPoint and PDF with unbranded sharing links on all plans. Its free Starter tier (100 AI credits/year, decks up to 20 slides) is enough to evaluate it properly; Pro is $20/mo billed annually. Like Gamma and Beautiful.ai, the editing lives on its platform rather than in Google Slides. Full breakdown: [Presentations.ai vs Slides by DocGPT.ai](/presentations-ai-vs-slides-ai-docgpt/).

## How to choose

1. **Slides-native or platform?** If your decks must live in Google Drive with normal Slides collaboration, pick an add-on (Slides by DocGPT.ai, Plus AI, MagicSlides, SlidesAI). If you're happy to present from a web link, Gamma and Presentations.ai open up.
2. **What do your decks start from?** Topics and your own text → Slides by DocGPT.ai. Long documents → Plus AI Pro. YouTube videos, PDFs, URLs → MagicSlides.
3. **How many decks per month?** Under 10 → Slides by DocGPT.ai's free tier covers you. High volume → compare unlimited tiers (MagicSlides Premium Plus, SlidesAI Premium) against your actual usage.
4. **Test before paying.** Run the same real topic through two or three free tiers/trials and judge the drafts, not the marketing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best AI presentation maker for Google Slides?

For working directly inside Google Slides, use a Workspace add-on: Slides by DocGPT.ai (10 free presentations/month, from $14.99/mo, 40+ languages), Plus AI (best template polish, from $10/mo billed annually), MagicSlides (best input-format breadth, from $29/mo), or SlidesAI (cheapest paid entry, from $10/mo). Standalone platforms like Gamma and Beautiful.ai make excellent decks but require you to create and edit outside Google Slides.

### Can Gamma or Beautiful.ai create decks inside Google Slides?

No. Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Tome (now discontinued), and Presentations.ai are standalone platforms. They can export PPTX files that Google Slides can import, but generation and editing happen on their own platforms, and imported decks may need layout cleanup. Only add-ons — Slides by DocGPT.ai, Plus AI, MagicSlides, SlidesAI — generate native Google Slides decks.

### Is there a free AI presentation maker for Google Slides?

Yes. Slides by DocGPT.ai includes 10 free presentations per month as a Google Slides add-on. SlidesAI's free plan allows 12 presentations per year, and MagicSlides is free to start (check its site for current limits). Gamma and Presentations.ai also have free tiers, but they work outside Google Slides.
