## Who looks for a Streak mail merge alternative?

Many people find Streak because they work inside Gmail. But some teams do not need a full CRM for the job in front of them. They need to:

- send a personalized campaign from a Google Sheet;
- keep recipient fields visible and editable;
- use Gmail as the sender;
- include useful personalization, links, and possibly attachments;
- track opens/clicks or campaign status where available;
- follow up without moving the whole workflow into a CRM.

If the list already lives in Sheets, a Sheets-native mail merge can be the simpler path.

## What Streak offers for mail merge

Streak is known as a Gmail-based CRM with pipeline and email workflow features. For some teams, bundling mail merge with a CRM is useful because outreach, deals, contacts, and follow-ups live in one system.

That same bundle can feel heavy if your goal is narrower: send a campaign from a spreadsheet, personalize rows, test, track, and follow up. In that case, evaluate whether you need CRM structure or a focused mail merge workflow.

## Where a Sheets-native add-on fits better

A Google Sheets-first workflow works well when your campaign data changes often:

1. **Build or import the list in Sheets.** Add email, name, company, segment, owner, and custom fields.
2. **Clean the data.** Remove duplicates, bounced addresses, and opted-out contacts.
3. **Draft a Gmail-style message.** Keep the copy personal and clear.
4. **Map merge fields.** Use tags such as `{{first_name}}`, `{{company}}`, and `{{custom_line}}`.
5. **Preview and test.** Catch empty fields, broken links, and attachment mistakes.
6. **Send responsibly.** Respect Gmail/Workspace limits and monitor replies and bounces.

## How to move a campaign list from a CRM to Sheets

If you are moving from a CRM-centered workflow to a spreadsheet-first one, start small:

1. Export only the contacts you are allowed to email.
2. Keep essential columns: email, first name, company, role, segment, owner, and status.
3. Add campaign-specific columns such as `custom_line`, `offer`, `last_contacted`, and `opt_out`.
4. Remove contacts with unclear consent or poor fit.
5. Run a test campaign with a small segment before expanding.

This makes your first send measurable without requiring a complete CRM migration.

## Sending limits and compliance cautions

A Streak alternative does not bypass Gmail or Workspace limits. Send to consented or clearly relevant contacts, include opt-out language where appropriate, test before launching, monitor bounces and replies, and avoid deceptive personalization. For attachments, verify that each recipient receives the correct file.
