## How it works {#how-it-works}

1. **Install the add-on.** Get [Slides by DocGPT.ai from the Google Workspace Marketplace](https://workspace.google.com/marketplace/app/create_presentation_with_ai_chatgpt_slid/517982554715). It runs inside Google Slides, so there is no separate app to learn.
2. **Open the generator.** In any Google Slides file, go to **Extensions → AI GPT for Slides → Start**.
3. **Describe the deck.** Enter a topic, choose the number of slides (10 for a classic investor deck), pick a writing tone (for example, confident and concise), and select a language.
4. **Paste your outline as source content.** This is the step most founders skip. Pasting your own pitch structure and facts into the additional-content field keeps the AI grounded in your company instead of generic startup boilerplate.
5. **Generate and edit.** The deck appears as normal Google Slides. Rearrange slides, rewrite bullets, apply your theme, and comment with cofounders exactly as you would in any deck. Export to PowerPoint or PDF via Google Slides itself (**File → Download**).

For a step-by-step walkthrough, see the docs: [create a presentation from a topic](/docs/slides-ai/create-presentation-from-topic) and [create slides from your own text](/docs/slides-ai/create-slides-from-text).

## Worked example: a 10-slide investor deck {#worked-example}

Settings to use in the add-on:

- **Slides:** 10
- **Tone:** professional and confident
- **Language:** English (or any of 40+)
- **Topic:** `Seed-stage investor pitch deck for Acme Robotics, warehouse automation robots for mid-size 3PL operators`

Then paste this into the source-content field, edited for your company. The bracketed placeholders are deliberate — fill them with real numbers before you generate, or after, in the editable deck:

```
Company: Acme Robotics — plug-and-play warehouse automation robots for
mid-size 3PL operators.

Follow this 10-slide structure:
1. Title — Acme Robotics: warehouse automation for the mid-market.
2. Problem — mid-size warehouses can't afford enterprise automation
   projects, and labor shortages are getting worse.
3. Solution — picking robots that deploy in days, not months, with no
   warehouse redesign.
4. Market — [insert your TAM / SAM / SOM figures and sources].
5. Product — how it works in 3 steps: map the aisle, train the picker,
   go live.
6. Traction — [insert real revenue, pilots, LOIs, growth rate].
7. Business model — robotics-as-a-service subscription per robot
   per month.
8. Competition — enterprise vendors vs. us on cost and deployment speed.
9. Team — [founders' names and the experience that earns the right
   to win].
10. The ask — raising [amount] to reach [specific milestone].
```

Generate, and you get a first draft of all 10 slides in native Google Slides. Because the deck is fully editable, the iteration loop with cofounders happens where it should: rewrite the problem slide, drop in your real traction chart, and re-order slides for the specific investor meeting — no re-generating, no export/import round trips.

## Where founders use it

- **First seed deck** — get from blank page to a complete structure in one sitting, then spend your time on the story, not the scaffolding.
- **Demo day variants** — regenerate the same outline at 5 slides for a 3-minute demo-day format, then tighten by hand.
- **Investor update decks** — a monthly update follows a repeatable structure; paste last month's notes as source content.
- **Partnership and BD decks** — reuse the product and traction slides, swap the problem framing per audience.

## What the AI drafts — and what founders must add {#honest-limits}

Be realistic about the division of labor. Slides by DocGPT.ai is genuinely good at the part founders procrastinate on: turning an outline into a complete, coherently structured deck with clean slide copy in your chosen tone. It will not know your real numbers. Market size, revenue, pilot results, team bios, and the size of your ask must come from you — that is why the worked example above uses explicit `[placeholders]` instead of letting the AI improvise figures. Investors notice invented stats, and an AI-generated market-size claim is not diligence-proof. Treat the generated deck as a strong first draft: the structure and copy are done, and your job shifts to plugging in real evidence and rehearsing the narrative.
