## Short answer {#short-answer}

**Plus AI and Slides by DocGPT.ai are both Google Workspace add-ons**, so with either one the deck you generate is a native, editable Google Slides file in your own Drive — no new platform to learn, and normal Google sharing, comments, and version history apply.

The practical differences are in pricing model and workflow. Slides by DocGPT.ai includes a **free tier of 10 presentations per month** and paid plans from **$14.99/mo**; you give it a topic (or paste your own text), choose slide count, writing tone, and one of 40+ languages, and it generates the full deck. Plus AI has no permanent free plan listed — its pricing page offers a **7-day free trial** for new customers — with paid plans from **$10/mo billed annually ($15 month-to-month)**, and it is known for polished template design; higher tiers add document uploads, very long prompts, and brand controls.

## When to choose Plus AI {#when-plus-ai}

Plus AI is a strong pick if:

- **Template polish is your top priority.** Plus AI has built its reputation on professional, consistent slide layouts, and its Team plan adds brand customization and shared team libraries.
- **You work across both Google Slides and PowerPoint.** It runs as an extension in both, so mixed Microsoft/Google teams can standardize on one tool.
- **You generate decks from long documents.** The Pro plan supports document uploads and prompts beyond 100K characters, which suits report-to-deck workflows.
- **You need high volumes with team features.** Team ($30/mo billed annually) and Max ($200/mo billed annually, unlimited AI credits) tiers target heavier organizational use.

## When to choose Slides by DocGPT.ai {#when-docgpt}

Slides by DocGPT.ai is the better fit if:

- **You want a real free tier, not just a trial.** 10 free presentations per month is enough to cover occasional deck-building indefinitely — no card, no 7-day clock.
- **You live in Google Slides.** The add-on generates complete decks natively in Slides, so collaboration, sharing, versioning, and Drive ownership work exactly as they always have.
- **You want simple, direct controls.** Enter a topic or paste your own text, set slide count, pick a writing tone and one of 40+ languages, and generate. The result is a normal Slides file you edit immediately.
- **You want predictable pricing.** Paid plans start at $14.99/mo — see [current plans](/google-slide-ai/#pricing). It is part of the DocGPT.ai suite used by 500,000+ users.

## Pricing {#pricing}

**Slides by DocGPT.ai** — free tier: 10 presentations/month. Paid plans from **$14.99/mo**; details on the [pricing page](/google-slide-ai/#pricing).

**Plus AI** (per its official pricing page, July 2026):

- **Basic** — $10/mo billed annually ($15 month-to-month): presentation creation, 1,500 monthly credits, slide editing.
- **Pro** — $20/mo billed annually ($25 month-to-month): adds document uploads, prompts up to 100K+ characters, AI image generation.
- **Team** — $30/mo billed annually ($40 month-to-month): 6,000 monthly credits, brand customization, shared team libraries.
- **Max** — $200/mo billed annually ($240 month-to-month): unlimited AI credits, priority support.
- **Trial** — 7 days with 1,000 AI credits, new customers only.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Plus AI or Slides by DocGPT.ai better for Google Slides?

Both are Google Slides add-ons that produce native, editable decks, so neither forces you onto a new platform. Slides by DocGPT.ai is stronger on entry cost — a free tier of 10 presentations per month and paid plans from $14.99/mo — with direct controls for slide count, tone, and 40+ languages. Plus AI is stronger on template polish, PowerPoint support, and document-upload workflows on its Pro plan and above.

### Does either tool have a free plan?

Slides by DocGPT.ai includes a permanent free tier of 10 presentations per month. Plus AI's pricing page lists a 7-day free trial with 1,000 AI credits for new customers rather than a permanent free plan; its paid plans start at $10/mo billed annually.

### Do the generated decks stay editable in Google Slides?

Yes. Both tools generate native Google Slides content, so you edit every slide with the normal Slides tools, the file lives in your Google Drive, and you can export to PPTX or PDF through Google Slides' built-in export.
