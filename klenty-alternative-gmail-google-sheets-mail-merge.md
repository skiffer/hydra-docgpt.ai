## Why people look for a Klenty alternative

People typically start shopping for an alternative when:

- their contact list already lives in **Google Sheets**, and they would rather not manage it inside a separate platform;
- they want personalized Gmail sending without adopting a broader multichannel sequencing suite;
- they need a simpler setup for a solo founder, a single SDR, or a small sales team;
- they are weighing cost against how many platform features they will realistically use.

None of this makes Klenty a poor product — it just means the job some people are hiring for is "send a personalized campaign from my Sheet," not "run a full multichannel sales-engagement platform."

## Klenty in context

Klenty is generally positioned as an **AI-assisted sales-engagement and cold-outreach platform**, with features oriented around list building, multi-step and multichannel cadences, and team prospecting workflows — a tier similar to other dedicated outreach suites. As of 2026 its paid plans are billed per user per month, but pricing and features change over time, so confirm the current details on Klenty's own site before deciding.

If you need a dedicated outbound engine with adaptive cadences and multichannel playbooks across a sales team, a platform like that may be the right call. If your real need is personalized Gmail sending from a spreadsheet, a mail merge tool is a closer fit.

## How the Mail Merge workflow works

1. Open the Google Sheet that holds your list and merge fields.
2. Install and open **Mail Merge for Gmail and Google Sheets**.
3. Map your columns to merge tags such as `{{first_name}}` and `{{company}}`.
4. Write a Gmail template that reads naturally when personalized.
5. Send a **test message** to yourself and a teammate and review every merge field.
6. Send in small batches first, then scale while monitoring replies and bounces.
7. Add follow-up sends for recipients who have not replied.

## What you keep, and what you trade

Moving from a full platform to a Sheets-native mail merge, you keep personalized sending, attachments, tracking, and follow-ups, and you keep your own Gmail address as the sender so replies land in your inbox. You trade away built-in multichannel cadences, automated sequence logic, and platform-managed sending infrastructure.

For many small teams that trade is worthwhile: the spreadsheet they already maintain becomes the campaign, with far less setup. For high-volume outbound teams that depend on cadences and rotation, a dedicated suite may still earn its cost.

## Who each tool suits best

A sales-engagement suite suits teams running scaled, multichannel outbound where sequencing, warm-up, and shared analytics justify the price. A Sheets-native mail merge suits founders, recruiters, and small teams who want personalized Gmail campaigns from the spreadsheet they already keep — with a lighter learning curve.

## A note on responsible outreach

Cold outreach carries responsibilities. Email people who have a genuine reason to hear from you, identify yourself honestly, include an easy way to opt out, and respect daily Gmail/Workspace sending limits. Genuine personalization and sensible pacing protect both your deliverability and your recipients' trust. Rules such as CAN-SPAM and GDPR may apply depending on where you and your recipients are.
