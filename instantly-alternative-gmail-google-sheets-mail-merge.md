# An Instantly Alternative for Gmail Mail Merge from Google Sheets

If your prospect list already lives in Google Sheets, a full cold-email stack can be more than your outreach actually needs. **Mail Merge for Gmail and Google Sheets** lets you send personalized Gmail campaigns straight from a spreadsheet — a focused, Workspace-native option for people searching for an **Instantly alternative** built around mail merge.

## Why people look for an Instantly alternative

People typically start shopping for an alternative when:

- their contact list already lives in **Google Sheets**, and they would rather not rebuild it inside a separate platform;
- they want personalized Gmail sending without adopting a full cold-email suite with lead databases and inbox warm-up;
- they need a simpler setup for a solo founder, a single SDR, or a small sales team;
- they are weighing monthly cost against how many platform features they will realistically use.

None of this makes Instantly a poor product — it just means the job some people are hiring for is "send a personalized campaign from my Sheet," not "run a high-volume cold-email platform."

## Instantly in context

Instantly is generally positioned as a **high-volume cold-email platform**, with features oriented around automated sequences, a built-in B2B lead database, multiple connected inboxes with warm-up, and an AI-assisted CRM. As of 2026 its paid plans are billed monthly and scale up as you add sending and data features, but pricing and features change over time, so confirm the current details on Instantly's own site before deciding.

If you need a dedicated outbound engine that bundles lead sourcing, inbox rotation, and warm-up across a sales team, a platform like that may be the right call. If your real need is personalized Gmail sending from a spreadsheet, a mail merge tool is a closer fit.

## How the Mail Merge workflow works

1. Open the Google Sheet that holds your list and merge fields.
2. Install and open **Mail Merge for Gmail and Google Sheets**.
3. Map your columns to merge tags such as `{{first_name}}` and `{{company}}`.
4. Write a Gmail template that reads naturally when personalized.
5. Send a **test message** to yourself and a teammate and review every merge field.
6. Send in small batches first, then scale while monitoring replies and bounces.
7. Add follow-up sends for recipients who have not replied.

## What you keep, and what you trade

Moving from a full cold-email platform to a Sheets-native mail merge, you keep personalized sending, attachments, tracking, and follow-ups, and you keep your own Gmail address as the sender so replies land in your inbox. You trade away a built-in lead database, automated inbox warm-up, and multi-inbox rotation.

For many small teams that trade is worthwhile: the spreadsheet they already maintain becomes the campaign, with far less setup. For high-volume outbound teams that depend on sourcing and rotation, a dedicated suite may still earn its cost.

## Pricing and fit considerations

Compare what you will actually use. A platform that bundles a lead database, warm-up, and many inboxes prices for that scope, while a Sheets-native mail merge prices around the narrower job of personalized Gmail sending. If you already own your list and send from one mailbox, much of a cold-email stack may go unused. Confirm current pricing on each vendor's site, since plans change.

## Who each tool suits best

A cold-email platform suits teams running scaled, sourced outbound where lead data, warm-up, and inbox rotation justify the price. A Sheets-native mail merge suits founders, recruiters, and small teams who want personalized Gmail campaigns from the spreadsheet they already keep — with a lighter learning curve.

## A note on responsible outreach

Cold outreach carries responsibilities. Email people who have a genuine reason to hear from you, identify yourself honestly, include an easy way to opt out, and respect daily Gmail/Workspace sending limits. Genuine personalization and sensible pacing protect both your deliverability and your recipients' trust. Rules such as CAN-SPAM and GDPR may apply depending on where you and your recipients are.
