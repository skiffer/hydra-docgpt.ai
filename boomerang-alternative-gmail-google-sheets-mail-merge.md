## When Boomerang is the right tool vs when you need mail merge

| Workflow question | Boomerang-style inbox workflow | Mail Merge for Gmail & Sheets |
|---|---|---|
| Main job | Schedule individual emails, set reminders, and manage follow-ups from Gmail | Send personalized campaigns from rows in Google Sheets |
| Data source | The message or contact in your inbox | A spreadsheet or CSV with columns for each merge field |
| Best fit | One-to-one reminders and send-later work | Sales outreach, recruiting, customer updates, event reminders, and small email campaigns |
| Personalization | Written manually per message | Merge fields such as first name, company, role, offer, city, date, and custom note |
| Campaign view | Inbox-centered | Sheet-centered, so every row can carry status, segment, notes, and next action |

If your workflow starts with "I have 300 rows in a Sheet," a spreadsheet mail merge is usually easier to control than composing one message at a time.

## Build a Sheets-native Gmail campaign in 6 steps

1. **Create the recipient sheet.** Add columns for `email`, `first_name`, `company`, `segment`, `personal_note`, and `follow_up_date`.
2. **Clean the list.** Remove duplicates, invalid addresses, bounced contacts, and anyone who opted out.
3. **Write one focused Gmail template.** Keep the message short and map each personalization slot to a real Sheet column.
4. **Preview several rows.** Check empty fields, long company names, and unusual punctuation before sending.
5. **Send a test and then a small segment.** Verify subject line, merge fields, links, and reply handling.
6. **Track and follow up.** Use engagement and reply status to decide who gets a second touch.

## Example campaign templates you can run from Google Sheets

### Sales follow-up after a meeting

**Sheet columns:** `first_name`, `company`, `meeting_topic`, `next_step`, `case_study_link`.

**Template:**

> Hi {{first_name}}, thanks again for talking about {{meeting_topic}}. Based on what you shared about {{company}}, the next useful step is {{next_step}}. I also attached a short example here: {{case_study_link}}. Would it be useful to compare notes this week?

### Recruiting outreach from a candidate list

**Sheet columns:** `first_name`, `role`, `company`, `reason_for_fit`, `scheduler_link`.

**Template:**

> Hi {{first_name}}, I saw your work in {{role}} at {{company}} and thought the fit was interesting because {{reason_for_fit}}. If you are open to hearing about a related role, here is a quick scheduling link: {{scheduler_link}}.

### Customer renewal reminder

**Sheet columns:** `first_name`, `renewal_date`, `account_owner`, `plan`, `next_step`.

**Template:**

## Sending quality and compliance checklist

Gmail mail merge works best when it is conservative and relevant. Before sending:

- use consented, opted-in, or clearly relevant contacts;
- include opt-out language where appropriate and honor opt-outs quickly;
- respect Gmail and Google Workspace sending limits;
- avoid spammy subject lines, misleading personalization, or purchased lists;
- send tests to yourself and a small internal group;
- monitor bounces, replies, complaints, and unsubscribes.

A mail merge tool can personalize and send the campaign, but list quality and responsible sending decide whether people trust the message.

## Boomerang alternative FAQ

### Is Mail Merge for Gmail and Sheets a full Boomerang replacement?

No. Boomerang is primarily an inbox productivity tool for scheduling, reminders, and follow-up prompts. Mail Merge for Gmail and Sheets is for sending personalized campaigns from spreadsheet rows. Choose based on the job you need to complete.

### When should I use a mail merge instead of a Gmail reminder tool?

Use mail merge when you have many recipients in a Sheet, each row needs personalization, and you want a repeatable send/test/track/follow-up process. Use a reminder tool when the work is mostly one-to-one inbox management.

### Can I schedule follow-ups from a spreadsheet campaign?

You can plan follow-ups from Sheet columns and engagement status, then send the next touch as a separate personalized campaign. Keep the timing respectful and avoid sending to people who opted out or already replied.

### Does mail merge bypass Gmail sending limits?

No. Any Gmail-based sending workflow must respect Gmail and Google Workspace limits. Segment your list, send tests, and increase volume carefully.
