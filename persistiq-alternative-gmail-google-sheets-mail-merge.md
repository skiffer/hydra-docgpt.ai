## Short answer: use mail merge for spreadsheet-first outreach

Mail Merge is a fit when your immediate campaign is smaller, more manual, and easier to manage from a Sheet:

- the prospect list is already in Google Sheets or a CSV export;
- the team wants to review every row before sending;
- the message uses a few practical fields such as `persona`, `pain_point`, `source`, and `CTA`;
- you need a Gmail-style send with test messages and batch control;
- replies and follow-ups can be tracked in the Sheet.

For many founders, consultants, agencies, recruiters, and small sales teams, the first campaign does not need a full sales engagement system. It needs clean data, relevant copy, and a controlled send.

## When a sales engagement platform is still the better choice

Choose a sales engagement platform when you need managed cadences, advanced team workflows, prospecting systems, CRM automation, deep reporting, multi-step sequence operations, or broader platform controls. Mail Merge is a lightweight Gmail + Sheets workflow for reviewed campaigns, not a replacement for a full SDR operating system.

## Build the campaign Sheet

A useful outbound Sheet should make relevance explicit:

| Column | Example | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `email` | `jamie@example.com` | Recipient |
| `first_name` | `Jamie` | Greeting |
| `company` | `BrightOps` | Account context |
| `persona` | `sales ops lead` | Message angle |
| `source` | `conference list` | Why the row exists |
| `pain_point` | `manual handoffs` | Personalized reason |
| `cta` | `see a workflow example` | Next step |
| `owner` | `Nina` | Sender or rep |
| `status` | `ready`, `sent`, `replied`, `skipped` | Campaign control |

If you cannot fill the context columns honestly, do not send yet. Research the account or remove the row.

## Draft personalized Gmail messages with merge fields

A small outbound email can be useful without sounding automated:

Subject: `Question about {{pain_point}} at {{company}}`

Hi {{first_name}},

I am reaching out because {{source}} suggested {{company}} may be dealing with {{pain_point}}.

I have a short example that could help a {{persona}} evaluate the workflow without a big setup.

Would it be useful if I sent it over?

Best,
{{sender_name}}

Preview rows before sending. Check that every sentence still makes sense when merge fields are replaced by real values.

## Review, send in batches, and handle replies/follow-ups

Use a conservative process:

1. **Segment intentionally.** Group recipients by use case, persona, or source.
2. **Review every row.** Remove weak-fit contacts and anyone who opted out.
3. **Send test messages.** Confirm subject lines, body copy, merge fields, links, and reply behavior.
4. **Launch a small batch.** Respect Gmail and Google Workspace limits.
5. **Watch bounces and replies.** Pause if quality looks poor.
6. **Plan respectful follow-ups.** Use Sheet statuses and notes rather than blasting everyone again.

### When is Mail Merge a good PersistIQ alternative workflow?

It can be a good fit when the campaign list is already in Google Sheets, you want reviewed Gmail personalization, and you do not need a full sales engagement platform for this send.

### Does Mail Merge replace sales engagement cadences?

No. Mail Merge helps with spreadsheet-first Gmail campaigns. Choose a sales engagement platform when you need advanced cadences, CRM automation, team analytics, or broader platform management.

### How do I keep outreach responsible?

Use consented or clearly relevant recipients, avoid purchased or spammy lists, include opt-out language where appropriate, test sends, respect Gmail/Workspace limits, and monitor bounces and replies.

Yes. Use status, reply, follow-up due, owner, and notes columns so the campaign remains understandable after the first send.
