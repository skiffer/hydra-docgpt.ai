## How each personalizes from Google Sheets

The shared model is the same: a Google Sheet holds one recipient per row, with columns such as email, first name, and company. You write a template with merge fields that pull from those columns, then send personalized copies from Gmail. GMass leans toward power users with larger lists and more sending controls; Mailmeteor emphasizes a clean, approachable interface. A focused add-on like Mail Merge for Gmail and Sheets keeps the Sheet itself as the single source of truth and concentrates on the merge-and-send job.

## How to run a Gmail mail merge from Sheets in 5 steps

1. **Prepare your Sheet.** One recipient per row; columns for email, first name, and any personalization fields.
2. **Write your template.** Add merge fields (for example `{{First name}}`) where they add real context.
3. **Preview rows.** Check empty fields and unusual names so nothing reads as robotic.
4. **Send a test, then a segment.** Validate rendering before the full list.
5. **Track and follow up.** Watch opens and replies, then send a timed follow-up to non-openers.
