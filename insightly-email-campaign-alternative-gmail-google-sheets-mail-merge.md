# Insightly Email Campaign Alternative for Gmail + Google Sheets Mail Merge

If you are looking for an **Insightly email campaign alternative**, you may have a CRM segment, project list, opportunity list, or contact export that needs one practical send. You do not always need a full campaign migration to send a reviewed personalized Gmail campaign.

**Mail Merge for Gmail and Google Sheets** lets you prepare the list in Sheets, merge safe CRM fields into a Gmail draft, test the campaign, send in batches, and track replies and follow-up status row by row.

## When exporting a CRM segment to Sheets is the faster path

A Gmail + Sheets campaign can be useful when:

- the audience is a focused project, account, renewal, sales, or customer segment;
- a teammate needs to review each row before sending;
- the message should come from a real Gmail or Google Workspace inbox;
- the campaign is short-lived and easy to track with Sheet columns;
- you want the CRM preserved as source of truth while sending a lightweight campaign.

This is an export-and-review workflow. Keep CRM IDs, owners, statuses, and context columns so important outcomes can be updated later.

## When Insightly or a CRM email platform is still better

Use a CRM or dedicated campaign platform when you need CRM-governed subscription state, project or opportunity reporting, dashboards, automation, team permissions, marketing modules, or complex lifecycle journeys. Mail Merge is for focused spreadsheet-reviewed Gmail sends, not a replacement for your CRM system of record.

## Prepare your Sheet: contact, company, project/opportunity, owner, context, next step

Use only the fields that help this campaign. A smaller, cleaner Sheet is safer than a giant export.

| Column | Example | Why it helps |
|---|---|---|
| `email` | taylor@example.com | Recipient |
| `first_name` | Taylor | Greeting |
| `company` | Beacon Services | Account context |
| `project_or_opportunity` | renewal review | Relevant topic |
| `owner` | Mateo | Internal owner |
| `last_interaction` | onboarding call | Reason to write |
| `next_step` | schedule a review | CTA |
| `do_not_contact` | false | Suppression |
| `send_status` | ready | Batch control |
| `crm_update_needed` | yes | Post-send workflow |

Remove unsubscribed, bounced, do-not-contact, duplicate, or unclear records before sending. Back up exports and avoid destructive imports or overwrites.

## Write Gmail copy with safe CRM merge fields

Subject: `Next step for {{company}}`

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> I wanted to follow up on {{project_or_opportunity}} after {{last_interaction}}.
>
> Would it be useful to {{next_step}} this week?
>
> Best,
> {{owner}}

If a field is incomplete or too sensitive, do not merge it. A clear, simple note is better than a broken or overly specific template.

## Test, send, monitor replies, and update CRM records

A responsible CRM-export campaign has a checklist:

1. Confirm the recipient list is consented, opted-in, or clearly relevant.
2. Remove suppressed, bounced, invalid, and duplicate addresses.
3. Review merge fields for accuracy and recipient sensitivity.
4. Send test emails for multiple segments.
5. Check links, attachments, subject lines, sender identity, and opt-out language.
6. Send in small batches and respect Gmail/Workspace sending limits.
7. Monitor replies, bounces, and complaints immediately.
8. Update CRM records, owners, stages, and follow-up notes through your normal process.

Do not use a mail merge to bypass compliance rules, CRM subscription status, or deliverability limits.

## Insightly email campaign alternative FAQ

### Is Mail Merge for Gmail and Sheets a full Insightly replacement?

No. Mail Merge is a Gmail + Google Sheets campaign workflow. It does not replace CRM records, project management, automation, dashboards, integrations, reporting, or team governance.

### Can I send a campaign from an Insightly export?

Yes, if your team can export or prepare the segment. Preserve useful CRM IDs, owner, status, and context fields, clean the list in Google Sheets, test the Gmail draft, and track outcomes.

### When should I use a CRM or campaign platform instead?

Use a CRM or campaign platform when you need centralized reporting, subscription governance, complex automation, project/opportunity history, marketing modules, or team workflows that must stay inside the CRM.

### What fields should I include in the Sheet?

Start with email, first name, company, project or opportunity, owner, last interaction, next step, do-not-contact status, send status, and CRM update needed.

### How do I keep a CRM-export campaign safe?

Use consented or clearly relevant contacts, honor suppression fields, test before sending, avoid purchased or spammy lists, respect Gmail/Workspace limits, and monitor bounces and replies.
